This page is dedicated to Starfish, an ambiguous and fascinating creature. Despite it's name it is not classified a fish!

Starfish or sea stars are star-shaped echinoderms belonging to the class Asteroidea.

About 1,900 species of starfish live on the seabed in all the world's oceans, from warm, tropical zones to frigid, polar regions. They are found from the intertidal zone down to abyssal depths, at 6,000 m (20,000 ft) below the surface.

Starfish are among the most famous and dramatic examples, being able to grow an entire new body from just a single arm. Starfish also have the ability to regenerate neurons, something not seen in many animals.
